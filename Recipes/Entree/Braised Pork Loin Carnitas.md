### Ingredients
---

#### Dry Brine (night before)
- 4.5–5 lb [[pork loin]], cut into 4 equal chunks (~3" × 5" × 4")
- 1 tbsp [[kosher salt]]

#### Spice Rub (day of)
- 1 tbsp [[cumin]]
- 1 tbsp [[chili powder]]
- 2 tsp [[smoked paprika]]
- 1 tsp [[oregano]] (Mexican oregano if you have it)
- 1 tsp [[garlic powder]]
- 1/2 tsp [[black pepper]]

#### Braise
- 2 tbsp [[olive oil]] (for searing)
- 2 cups [[chicken broth]]
- Juice of 2 [[limes]] (or 1 [[orange]] + 1 lime)
- 1 medium [[onion]], diced
- 5 cloves [[garlic]], smashed
- 2 [[bay leaves]]
- 2–3 tbsp [[butter]] (compensates for loin's low fat content)
- 1 canned [[chipotle pepper]] in adobo + 1 tsp adobo sauce (optional, adds smoke/heat)

#### For Crisping
- Reserved braising liquid
- 1 tbsp [[olive oil]] or [[pork fat]]

---

### Directions

#### Night Before — Dry Brine
1. Cut pork loin into 4 roughly equal chunks along the long axis.
2. Salt generously on all sides with kosher salt.
3. Place on a rack over a plate, uncovered, in the fridge overnight (8-24 hours).
4. Do NOT add spices yet — just salt.

#### Day Of — Sear & Braise
1. Remove pork from fridge. Pat dry with paper towels. Mix spice rub and apply generously to all sides.
2. Heat olive oil in a heavy skillet (cast iron or stainless) over medium-high until shimmering.
3. Sear chunks on all sides, 2-3 minutes per side, until deep golden brown. Don't rush this — the crust is flavor. Work in batches if needed.
4. Transfer seared pork to a deep oven-safe roasting pan or dutch oven.
5. Deglaze the skillet with a splash of the chicken broth, scraping up fond. Pour over the pork.
6. Add remaining broth, citrus juice, onion, garlic, bay leaves, butter, and chipotle (if using).
7. Liquid should come about halfway up the pork chunks. Add more broth if needed.
8. Cover tightly — lid or double layer of foil crimped tight around the rim. Steam must stay in.
9. Oven at **300°F** for **3.5–4 hours**.

#### Checking Doneness
- **3 hour mark:** Check internal temp and do a fork test. Stick a fork in and twist.
  - Below 190°F or resists shredding → keep going, check every 30 min.
  - 195–205°F and shreds easily → done.
- **Do NOT crank the oven temp if it's slow.** Low and slow is the whole point.
- If liquid has reduced significantly, add 1/2 cup broth.
- **Flip chunks once** at the halfway point (~1.5–2 hours).

#### Shred & Crisp
1. Remove pork from braising liquid. Discard bay leaves.
2. Shred with two forks — it should fall apart easily.
3. Return shredded pork to braising liquid. Stir to coat. **Let it soak 10 minutes.** This is where the moisture and flavor absorb. Do not skip.
4. Strain the liquid and reserve (great for reheating leftovers).
5. Spread shredded pork on a sheet pan or cast iron skillet in a thin layer.
6. Drizzle lightly with oil.
7. Broil on high for 3-5 minutes until edges are crispy and caramelized. Watch carefully — goes from perfect to burned fast.
8. Toss the crispy bits with the softer underneath meat. Squeeze fresh lime over the top.

### Serving Suggestions
- Lettuce wraps with [[Serrano Crema]], [[queso fresco]], pickled onion, cilantro
- Low-carb tortillas with same toppings
- Over [[cauliflower rice]] with [[Roasted Green Chile Sauce]]
- [[Carnitas-stuffed Bell Peppers]] (use this as the carnitas base)
- Naked, with a fork, standing at the counter (no judgment)

### Notes
- **Why butter in a braise?** Pork loin is very lean — unlike shoulder, it has almost no intramuscular fat. The butter emulsifies into the braising liquid and gets absorbed when you soak the shredded meat. It's replacing the fat that a shoulder would have naturally.
- **Leftovers:** Store shredded meat in reserved braising liquid. Reheat in a skillet — re-crisp under broiler as needed. Stays good 4-5 days in fridge, freezes well for months.
- **Timing:** Plan 4.5-5 hours from sear to table. Start early — finished pork holds great in covered liquid. Late pork means late dinner.
- **Don't skip the soak.** Shredding and immediately crisping = dry. Shredding, soaking in liquid, THEN crisping = juicy with crispy edges.
- 🔥 For extra heat, add a second chipotle or a few dashes of your favorite hot sauce to the braising liquid.
