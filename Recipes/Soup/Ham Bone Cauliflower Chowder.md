# Ingredients
---
## Ham Broth
- 1 [[ham bone]] with attached meat
- 2 [[yellow onion]], quartered
- 3 stalks [[celery]], rough chopped
- 2 [[carrots]], rough chopped
- 4 cloves [[garlic]], smashed
- 3 [[green onions]], rough chopped
- 2 [[bay leaf]]
- 1 tsp [[black peppercorns]]
- 1 tsp [[dried thyme]]
- 1 tbsp [[apple cider vinegar]]
- [[water]] to cover

## Chowder
- 2 tbsp [[butter]]
- 1 [[yellow onion]], diced
- 3 stalks [[celery]], diced
- 4 cloves [[garlic]], minced
- 6 cup [[ham broth]]
- 24 oz [[cauliflower]], diced (frozen is fine)
- 2 to 3 cup chopped [[ham]] from the bone
- 1 tsp [[dried thyme]]
- 0.5 tsp [[black pepper]]
- 1 tsp [[sea salt]], or to taste
- 1 tbsp [[dijon mustard]]
- 4 oz [[cream cheese]]
- 0.5 cup [[heavy cream]]
- 1 cup [[sharp cheddar cheese]], shredded
- 2 tbsp [[parsley]], chopped (optional)
- [[hot sauce]] to taste (optional)
---

# Directions
1. Make the broth. Put the ham bone, onions, celery, carrots, garlic, green onions, bay leaves, peppercorns, thyme, vinegar, and enough water to cover in a large pot. Bring just to a boil, then immediately reduce to a gentle simmer. Skim any foam from the top.

2. Simmer the broth gently for 3 to 5 hours, partially covered, until the broth is rich and the ham on the bone gives up easily.

3. Pull the bone and let it cool enough to handle. Pick off the usable ham, chop it, and reserve for the chowder. Strain the broth and discard the spent solids.

4. Start the chowder base. In a soup pot or Dutch oven, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the diced onion and celery and cook until softened, about 6 to 8 minutes. Add the garlic and cook 1 minute more.

5. Add the broth, cauliflower, chopped ham, thyme, black pepper, salt, and Dijon. Bring to a simmer and cook until the cauliflower is very tender, about 15 to 20 minutes.

6. Build the chowder texture. Use an immersion blender to blend part of the soup, leaving plenty of chunks behind, or scoop out 2 to 3 cups, blend them, and return them to the pot. You want chowder texture, not baby food.

7. Finish it. Lower the heat. Stir in the cream cheese until smooth, then add the heavy cream and cheddar. Stir until the cheese is melted and the chowder is rich and unified. Taste and adjust salt, pepper, and hot sauce if using.

8. Serve. Top with parsley if desired. This gets even better after a night in the fridge.
